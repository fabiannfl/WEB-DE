About me

Since I was a small child I have been interested in how the things that took apart all my toys worked, what I like the most is learning by doing, in fact, if I don't understand the context I hardly assimilate it, I have been experimenting with tools and materials. Learn to use and implement them. Now, that's all I've been doing: making things and teaching how to make things with metal, wood, plastic, and more.I've been making stuff since I could hold a hammer or screwdriver and probably longer than that. I grew up with my mother, who offered me the freedom to experiment and explore. She put me in an environment to continue experimenting and learning, so my education, which has been mostly self-taught, involved a wide range of problem solving for this reason I like the difficult using all kinds of tools, skills and materials ... and that is my philosophy in the workshop; learn as much as you can about anything and everything so that it becomes part of your problem solving arsenal the root of creativity and necessity is the mother of all inventions
